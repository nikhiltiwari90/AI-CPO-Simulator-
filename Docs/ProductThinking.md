# Product Thinking
## Why the AI CPO Simulator Matters
**Author:** Nikhil Tiwari

---

## The Problem This Simulates

Every product organisation, at some point, faces a moment like this:

> A key metric drops. Retention falls. Conversion craters. Engagement declines.

What happens next, in most companies, is **slow and fragmented**:

- The data team takes days to produce a funnel analysis
- Customer support compiles ticket themes separately, often weeks later
- Competitive intelligence is an ad-hoc Slack thread, rarely structured
- Experimentation ideas get proposed without clear prioritisation logic
- The roadmap conversation happens in a separate meeting, disconnected from the root cause
- By the time an executive memo gets written, the analysis is stale and the narrative has drifted from the data

**The AI CPO Simulator collapses this entire process into a single, structured, sequential pipeline that completes in under 3 minutes.**

---

## Why I Built This as 6 Agents, Not 1

A single LLM call asked to "analyse this problem and write an executive memo" produces shallow, generic output — because it's trying to do five different jobs in one pass: data analysis, customer empathy, market awareness, experimental design, and strategic prioritisation.

**These are different cognitive modes.** A good product analyst thinks differently from a good UX researcher, who thinks differently from a good competitive strategist, who thinks differently from a good experimentation lead.

By decomposing the problem into 6 specialised agents — each with a narrow mandate and a specific framework toolkit — the system produces output with genuine depth in each dimension, then synthesises that depth into a coherent executive narrative.

**This mirrors how strong product organisations actually function**: specialised functions feeding into product leadership, not a single generalist trying to cover everything shallowly.

## Working Backwards From the Output

I designed this project by starting with the artifact I wanted to exist at the end: **a one-page Executive Product Review that a VP of Product would actually find useful in a board prep meeting.**

A real Executive Product Review needs:
- A clear, undebatable diagnosis of what went wrong
- Evidence that customer impact has been considered, not just metrics
- Awareness of competitive context — are we losing to someone, or is this internal?
- A forward plan: specific experiments, not vague "we'll investigate"
- A roadmap implication: what changes as a result of this finding
- A business impact projection: why should the VP care, in dollars or strategic terms

I mapped each of these six needs to one agent. **The agent architecture is not arbitrary — it's derived directly from the structure of a real executive artifact.**

## The Product Problem Domain I Chose to Simulate

I deliberately scoped the example prompts to **product metric crises** — retention drops, conversion declines, NPS crashes — rather than building a generic "ask AI anything about product" tool.

**Why a narrow domain:**

A tool that does one thing extremely well is more credible than a tool that claims to do everything adequately. By constraining the simulator to "diagnose a product metric crisis," every agent's prompt could be tightly specified, and the output quality is correspondingly higher than a more generic system would produce.

This is itself a product decision: **scope discipline over feature breadth**, which is exactly the kind of trade-off a PM has to make constantly.

## What This Project Does NOT Claim

I want to be explicit about the honest boundaries of this project, because overclaiming undermines credibility with technical interviewers:

- **This does not connect to real product analytics data** (Amplitude, Mixpanel, etc.) — it simulates the *type* of analysis a connected system would produce, based on the problem statement alone
- **The metrics in agent outputs are illustrative**, generated by the LLM to be realistic and structurally correct, not pulled from any real dataset
- **This is a demonstration of agentic AI orchestration and prompt engineering**, not a claim that LLM-generated analysis should replace real data science and real customer research

The honest framing in interviews: *"This demonstrates how I would architect an AI-augmented product intelligence system. In a real implementation, Agent 1 would query real analytics APIs, Agent 2 would pull real support ticket data via an integration, and so on. The orchestration architecture — sequential agents with compounding context — is the transferable insight, regardless of whether the underlying data source is simulated or real."*

---

## Connection to PM Craft

This project is, at its core, a demonstration of **synthesis under ambiguity** — the fundamental PM skill.

A vague signal ("retention dropped 18%") contains no inherent explanation. The job of a product leader is to systematically narrow that ambiguity: what broke, who's affected, what the market context is, what we should test, what we should build, and how we explain it upward.

Building a system that automates this synthesis process — even in simulated form — required me to deeply internalise the *structure* of good product reasoning, not just its vocabulary. That internalisation is the actual skill being demonstrated, more than the React code or the API integration.
